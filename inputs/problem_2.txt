Railway rescheduling problem -- junction area with route choice

Operational setting
Several train services pass through a busy junction area composed of a set
of track resources (block sections and switches). Each train has a default
itinerary, i.e. an ordered list of block sections it occupies from entry to
exit of the area, together with a minimum occupation (running) time per
block section and an entry time at which it becomes available at the area
boundary. For some trains an alternative route through the junction exists
that uses a different ordered set of block sections.

Decisions to be made
- For each train, which of its available routes through the junction is
  used (routing).
- The time at which each train starts occupying each block section on its
  chosen route (retiming).
- The relative order of trains that compete for the same block section
  (sequencing).

Objective
Minimise the total or maximum secondary delay measured at the exit of the
junction area, i.e. the delay caused by the rescheduling decisions on top
of any entry delay the trains already carry.

Operational rules that must hold
- A train may occupy a block section only after it has finished the
  preceding block section on its route (route continuity).
- A train occupies each block section for at least its minimum running
  time on that section.
- A block section can be occupied by at most one train at a time; a
  following train may enter only after a minimum separation (headway)
  from the moment the previous train released the section.
- A train cannot enter the area before its entry time.
- Exactly one route is selected per train.

Quantities the model should expose
Continuous variables for block-section entry times, binary route-selection
variables, and binary sequencing variables for trains sharing a block
section. The formulation is expected to be a mixed-integer linear program.
