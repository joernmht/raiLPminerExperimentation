Railway rescheduling problem -- double-track high-speed line, partial blockage

Operational setting
A set of train services runs on a double-track high-speed corridor that
connects an ordered sequence of stations. Under normal operation each
direction uses its own track. A disruption makes one track of a single
segment between two consecutive stations temporarily unavailable, so trains
of both directions must share the remaining track over that segment for a
known time window. Each train has a planned timetable: a scheduled departure
time at its origin, scheduled arrival/departure times at intermediate
stations, and a minimum running time on every segment plus a minimum dwell
time at every station where it stops.

Decisions to be made
- The order in which the affected train services traverse the single-track
  segment (a sequencing decision between conflicting trains).
- The adjusted arrival and departure time of every affected train at every
  station (retiming).
- Which train services, if any, are cancelled when the conflict cannot be
  resolved by delaying alone.

Objective
Recover operation with the least disturbance to passengers: minimise a
weighted sum of total train delay (deviation of actual times from the
planned timetable) and a penalty for each cancelled service.

Operational rules that must hold
- A train cannot depart a station earlier than its planned departure time
  and cannot run a segment faster than its minimum running time.
- A train must dwell at least its minimum dwell time at every stop.
- Two trains using the blocked single-track segment must be separated by a
  minimum safety headway and may not occupy the segment in opposing
  directions at the same time.
- Trains using the same track in the same direction must keep a minimum
  headway at departure and arrival of every shared segment.
- A station can hold only a limited number of trains simultaneously
  (platform/track capacity).
- A cancelled train imposes no timing or headway requirements.

Quantities the model should expose
Continuous time variables for arrivals/departures, binary ordering
(sequencing) variables for pairs of conflicting trains, and binary
cancellation variables for services. The formulation is expected to be a
mixed-integer linear program.
