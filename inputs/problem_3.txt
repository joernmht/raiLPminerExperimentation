Railway rescheduling problem -- corridor with ordered stations and stops

Operational setting
A set of trains operates on a line described by an ordered sequence of
stations connected by segments. Every train has a fixed stopping pattern
(the subset of stations at which it stops), a planned departure time from
its origin, a minimum running time on each segment it traverses, and a
minimum dwell time at each station where it stops. Trains run in a single
direction on this corridor. A disturbance has delayed the departure of one
or more trains, propagating conflicts down the corridor.

Decisions to be made
- The actual departure and arrival time of every train at every station on
  its path (retiming).
- The passing order of trains on each segment when their nominal schedules
  conflict (sequencing / overtaking is only allowed at stations).

Objective
Minimise the total weighted delay of all trains at their final destination,
where each train may carry a priority weight.

Operational rules that must hold
- Actual departure from the origin is not earlier than the planned
  departure time.
- Running time on every segment is at least the minimum running time.
- Dwell time at every stop is at least the minimum dwell time.
- Consecutive trains on the same segment keep at least a minimum headway at
  both ends of the segment, consistent with their passing order.
- Overtaking between two trains may change only at a station, not in the
  middle of a segment.

Quantities the model should expose
Continuous arrival/departure time variables per train and station and
binary order variables for each pair of trains sharing a segment. The
formulation is expected to be a mixed-integer linear program.
